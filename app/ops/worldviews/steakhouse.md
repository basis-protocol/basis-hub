# Steakhouse Financial

## Worldview Summary

Expert advisory. Manual stablecoin reviews (Mountain Protocol USDM — the canonical deep-dive). Institutional-grade risk visibility. ENS endowment reporting as showcase of treasury analysis. Building Grove protocol for credit infrastructure. They do the thing Basis automates — but for one stablecoin at a time, by hand, with deep expertise.

## Key Themes

- **Expert judgment as moat:** Their value is the depth and quality of analysis. Seb Derivaux's reviews are the gold standard.
- **Manual doesn't scale:** They can review one stablecoin in detail (USDM review is 30+ pages). They can't review 17 stablecoins continuously.
- **ENS endowment as showcase:** Their ENS endowment management and reporting demonstrates treasury analysis at institutional grade.
- **Grove = credit infrastructure:** Building protocol-level credit infrastructure. Complementary to, not competitive with, stablecoin quality scoring.
- **Credibility through rigor:** Everything they publish is deeply researched. They don't do surface-level.

## Bridge Patterns

- "Data layer under expert judgment" — SII doesn't replace Steakhouse reviews, it provides the continuous monitoring layer that makes expert deep-dives more efficient. They focus on the 1-2 that matter most; SII watches all 17.
- "Scaling the Seb Derivaux methodology" — his approach is the right one. SII operationalizes a version of it that runs continuously.
- "ENS endowment stablecoin quality" — the ENS endowment holds stablecoins. What's the quality profile of that allocation? An exposure report is a natural extension of what they already do.
- "Methodology peer review" — invite them to critique the SII methodology. They're the most qualified reviewers. Their feedback makes the methodology better AND creates credibility.

## Language to Use

- methodology, expert judgment, rigorous analysis
- data layer, continuous monitoring, complement
- institutional quality, transparent methodology

## Language to Avoid

- automated (implies their manual work is inferior)
- replace, substitute (frame as complement, never replacement)
- faster, cheaper (this isn't about cost, it's about coverage and continuity)
